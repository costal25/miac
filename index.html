Welcome!

My lovely, older sister, Mia, is a great addition to my family. Some of you upperclassmen may know her, perhaps as "Mia Pasta" or any other variant of Italian food--how ironic that she now can no longer eat pasta unless it is gluten free.

I hope to find you all well and safe in these trying times. As someone who is quite terrible at dealing with stress, I vote we delve into the life of someone else who has been plagued with stress in these past couple years with the pandemic and all that mess--since why deal with our own stress when we can observe someone else's?

Now, of course, my sister's life is not all stress--there are quite a few wonderful parts of her daily life that are quite pleasant and nothing short of intriguing. I think that it's safe to say that she has such an interesting life that one could make a thousand documentaries about her! Howewver, since I am lazy, I will just be coding this website instead.


As wonderful as it would be if my sister still went to WHS, I think it is safe to say that she has moved on as an alumni. Granted, whenever she comes back to Massachusetts and we drive past the high school, she gets a bit of PTSD from her time here, but hey, don't we all have that one traumatic era during our studies? Either way, her current education at Northwestern is proving to be a much more suitable environment for my sister.


Now, here's a fun fact--did you know that Mia likes to run? Like, really likes to run--it's a bit absurd. I personally think that if it weren't for her unhealthy infatuation with the world of medicine and mathematics she would be running 24/7/365. But either way, she is the epitome of "she's a runner, she's a track star", because she truly is a track star. You can see all kinds of awards and things from her running career if you pay a little visit to WHS.



If I've got you hooked, but you don't feel like reading and/or you just want to see pictures of the magnificent Mia Pasta--who is allergic to gluten, I have a little gallery page for you. Happy viewing! Please enjoy this little introduction into my sister's life, and stay safe! :)
